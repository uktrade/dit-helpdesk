The UK has reached a trade agreement with the EU which is expected to take effect from 1 January 2021. This establishes zero tariffs or quotas on trade between the UK and EU, where goods meet the relevant rules of origin. Find out more about the agreement.

This agreement also provides for full bilateral cumulation, allowing EU inputs and processing to be counted as UK input in UK products exported to the EU, and vice versa.

Detailed rules of origin applicable from 1 January 2021 will be added to this service shortly, but can be seen in the full Trade and Cooperation Agreement text, in the Rules of Origin chapter (pages 27-41) and the annexes on Rules of Origin (pages 415-485). This also includes a light touch declaration process (set out from page 478). Find out more about rules of origin aspects of the agreement.

The UK's MFN tariff will apply if the conditions for preferential access are not met.
