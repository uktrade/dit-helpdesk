Rules of origin - {{ commodity.commodity_code|dotted_format }}

The rate of duty that must be paid on your goods will depend on 3 things:

  • whether the product is intended for use in the UK or the EU
  • the proportions of each shipment intended for use in the UK and the EU respectively
  • whether UK-intended products may subsequently be imported into the Republic of Ireland

Products are ‘non-originating’ if any of the materials used to make them are not wholly from or processed in the country of origin.

If your product is solely intended for use in the UK it may qualify for a UK tariff preference. However, if a proportion of the shipment is also intended to be used in the EU (such as Ireland), it may qualify for EU preferences, depending on the amount or type of product.

If you have a customs agent, freight forwarder or importer, they should be able to tell you which tariff preferences and rules of origin apply to your shipment. Otherwise, please contact the Trader Support Service for more information.

EU rules of origin

EU rules of origin will apply if any part of your shipment will be exported to Northern Ireland before being shipped on to an EU country (such as Ireland).

You can find more information by going to the EU’s Access2Markets website, and clicking the ‘Rules of origin’ section.
