Rules of origin for tariff preference - {{ commodity.commodity_code|dotted_format }}

Depending on where it is intended that this product be used, it may qualify for a tariff preference with either the UK or the EU.

Which (if either) tariff preference it qualifies for will depend on:

  • whether the product is intended for use in the UK or the EU
  • the proportions of each shipment intended for use in the UK and the EU respectively
  • whether UK-intended products may subsequently be imported into the Republic of Ireland

Non-originating products must meet the rules of origin for either the UK or the EU to qualify for a tariff preference. Non-originating products are those with any material not wholly from or processed in the country of origin.

If you have a customs agent, freight forwarder or importer, they should be able to tell you which tariffs and charges apply to your shipment. Otherwise, please contact the Trader Support Service for more information.
