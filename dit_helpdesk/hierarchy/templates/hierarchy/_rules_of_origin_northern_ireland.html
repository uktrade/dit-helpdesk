Rules of origin - {{ commodity.commodity_code|dotted_format }}

The rate of duty that must be paid on your goods will depend on:

  • the type of goods
  • where they are judged to have ‘originated’ from
  • the country the goods are being imported into

Products are ‘non-originating’ if any of the materials used to make them are not wholly from or processed in the country of origin.

Please follow the guidance on when goods are not at risk for detailed rules of origin information for Northern Ireland.

EU rules of origin

EU general rules of origin will apply if any part of your shipment is considered ‘at risk’ of entering an EU country (such as Ireland).

‘At risk’ goods must meet product-specific rules of origin to qualify for a tariff preference.

You can find more information by going to the EU’s Access2Markets website, and clicking the ‘Rules of origin’ section.
