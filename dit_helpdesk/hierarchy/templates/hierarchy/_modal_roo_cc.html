Change of Chapter (CC)

When a non-originating material is used to produce another finished product, the first 2 digits of its original commodity code will need to change once it’s incorporated into the final product. This is referred to as a change in chapter.

Example:

A UK food producer makes fresh pasta using durum wheat flour, water and egg. The flour is an ingredient that has been sourced from outside the UK. It must go through a change in tariff classification to gain UK originating status once it has been made into the pasta.

The 6-digit commodity code for durum wheat flour is 110100.

It is used to make fresh pasta, which has the commodity code 190211.

The fresh pasta’s commodity code is different at the 2-digit level (first 2 numbers) to that of durum wheat flour. So, once the flour has been made into the pasta, we say it has undergone a change in chapter.
