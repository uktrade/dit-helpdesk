{{ selected_origin_country_name }} Northern Ireland

When bringing goods into Northern Ireland from outside the UK and the EU, you will pay the UK duty rate if your goods are not ‘at risk’ of onward movement to the EU. However, goods ‘at risk’ of entering the EU’s single market will pay EU tariffs.

Guidance is available on when goods are not at risk, how commercial processing within Northern Ireland is defined and registration for the UK Trader Scheme. Guidance on any tariff waivers will follow shortly.

UK tariffs

When bringing goods into Northern Ireland from outside the UK and the EU, you will pay the UK duty rate if your goods are not ‘at risk’ of onward movement to the EU. Goods ‘at risk’ of entering the EU’s single market will pay EU tariffs.

Read guidance on whether goods are ‘at risk’ or not.

EU tariffs may apply where goods are exported to Northern Ireland for use in the EU (including Ireland), or are considered ‘at risk’ of being exported to the EU. These are shown in the table.

Meursing codes are used in the EU to apply variable specific duties to compound agri-goods (such as confectionery and bakery products), depending on the relative content of sugar, flour and milk starch in the product being imported.

You will need to work out the additional code using the Meursing code tool. To calculate the duty rate, enter the meursing code (without the 7 at the start) into the Meursing calculator to find variable duty elements such as AE, ADFM and ADSZ.
